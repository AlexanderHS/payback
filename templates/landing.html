A decision journal, ranked by what it's worth

Should I buy a car? Take up a hobby? Quit my job? Have a kid?

Payback turns the questions you keep half-asking yourself into a ranked list of what's actually worth doing — by forcing the boring discipline of writing down what you'd pay for the benefit, then doing the math.

§ 01 — One ranked list

Everything you've considered, in one place, ordered by value.

Each row is something you might do. Costs and benefits go in. The list reorders itself by payback period — how fast it earns back its upfront cost. Start at the top.

Sample data. Drop the bad ideas with one click — but keep them visible, so you remember why you ruled them out.

§ 02 — The mechanism

It works because it makes you do the writing.

  1. i.

    Capture

    Add a project. Name it, write a one-line target, sketch a strategy. List the alternatives you considered and why you rejected them — even putting down the bad ideas is illustrative.

  2. ii.

    Force WTP

    Put a number on the benefit, even if it's not money. What would you pay per year for a really nice vegetable garden? That number is now part of the math.

  3. iii.

    Rank

    Costs and benefits convert to a payback period. The list reorders itself. The discipline of filling in the fields is most of the value; the ranking is what makes you do it.

§ 03 — A worked example

A vegetable garden, end-to-end.

Vegetable garden

~47 mo
Target
A back-yard plot productive enough to cover most of our greens, year-round.
Strategy
Raised beds along the fence, drip irrigation, compost from kitchen waste. Spring weekends planting; maintenance-only after.
Alternatives
CSA box ($60 / wk), nothing. CSA gives the food without the time, but loses the WTP-of-having-a-garden bit.
Costs
  • Beds, soil, irrigation$ 1,200 once
  • Seeds, compost, replacements$ 25 / mo
  • Setup & ongoing time50 h once + 4 h / mo
Benefits
  • Replaces grocery vegetables$ 60 / mo
  • WTP for a really nice garden$ 500 / yr
Verdict
Payback in ~47 months. Marginal. Worth doing for the WTP, not the math — and now you've actually said that, on paper.

It's nice, from an executive-function perspective, to just have one place where everything you've considered is ranked by value. You start at the top, and work down.

§ 04 — A note for the AI-curious

There's an API. If that means something to you, you'll know what to do with it.

Each user can mint API keys. Hand one to an agent and let it do the tedious part — interrogating you about a vague idea until it has enough to log a real project. The discipline of what a project needs to contain is the durable value; the typing is the friction we'd all rather skip. 

$ curl https://payback.2ho.me/api/projects/next \
    -H "Authorization: Bearer $PAYBACK_KEY"

No agent? Ignore this section. The web app is the whole product.
